# Brand Voice — Craig Lappin-Smith

## The short version
Direct. Funny. A bit silly. Warm but never gushing. Smart without being smug. Never corporate.

## Tone by brand

### Coarse, Isla, Deli by Coarse
Full personality unlocked. Puns welcome. Jokes expected. Topical references, playful digs, self-aware humour. The writing should feel like it came from a person, not a brand.

### Crush (wine bar)
Still has character and wit — just slightly more refined. You can still make a joke, but you're not doing a Taylor Swift bit. Think smart and dry rather than silly.

## What good looks like
This newsletter excerpt is the benchmark for Coarse:

> *"This menu contains more hits than The Tortured Poets Department."*

> *"It's 3 months until the Olympics and it seems Ruari is already in the mood. We've never seen more nations named on a menu. English asparagus, French peas, Indian spices, Mexican vanilla, Scottish Greens. Oh wait, that last one has been cut out."*

> *"And to finish off, thanks to g̶l̶o̶b̶a̶l̶ w̶a̶r̶m̶i̶n̶g̶ the small bout of warm weather..."*

Key qualities in that writing:
- Topical but not forced
- Puns that are bad enough to be good
- Knows when to cut the joke and get back to the point
- Feels like a real person wrote it after a glass of wine, not a brand manager

## Inspiration
The Rochambeau Club — reference this when setting tone for anything more playful or members-club adjacent.

## What sounds wrong
Anything you'd find on a LinkedIn post. Specifically:
- "Elevate" / "elevated experience"
- "Curated" / "carefully curated"
- "Passionate about"
- "Journey"
- "Excited to announce"
- "Synergy" / "ecosystem"
- "Authenticity" used unironically
- Any sentence that could end with 🚀

Also wrong:
- Fluffy filler sentences that say nothing
- Over-explaining a joke
- Being try-hard — the humour should feel effortless, not desperate
- Formal sign-offs ("Kind regards", "Best wishes")

## Phrases that feel right
- Casual, warm sign-offs ("Say hello to my little friend, Team coarse xx")
- Short punchy sentences after a longer build-up
- Self-aware asides in brackets
- Using "we" for the business, "I" when it's clearly personal

## Format preferences for written content
- Short paragraphs
- No bullet points in customer-facing copy (prose only)
- Subject lines and headlines should have a hook — never just descriptive
